What is CAST?
Start here / What is CAST?

A bilateral coordination protocol for commercial payments.

CAST — Commercial Agreement Settlement Technology. Before a covered payment moves, both parties co-author one cryptographically bound record of the terms. That record becomes the authorization, and it travels with the payment through the ERP, the audit, the lender, and the insurer as tamper-evident proof.

The one-line thesis

The proof is produced once, at transaction time. Verification cost drops to near zero, because checking a hash is structurally cheaper than reconstructing a decision chain.

This matches your live hero exactly: require mutual authorization before payments move. The intellectual register sits one layer above the product — the architecture is vertical-agnostic; Confirm & Pay is its first instantiation.

What CAST is not
Start here / What CAST is not

What CAST is not.

The brief omitted this section. It's one of your most credible assets — it signals intellectual honesty and pre-empts the objections every skeptical reader brings. Put it near the top.

  • Not an ERP replacement. It sits alongside your existing systems and produces posting-ready exports with the lineage hash in the memo field.
  • Not workflow automation. Workflows are consequences of events and policies — not the primary design object.
  • Not a fraud guarantee. It eliminates unilateral fraud. Collusion becomes documented and detectable, not impossible.
  • Not a point solution. AP is the first instantiation of a vertical-agnostic architecture.
Precise claim (from the live site)

CAST proves the vendor confirmed these payment terms for this specific record at this timestamp. It does not claim the invoice is inherently real, and it does not eliminate collusion.

The five primitives
The primitives / The five primitives

The five primitives.

Everything else is a projection of these five objects. These definitions are taken verbatim in substance from your live site so the hub and the homepage never disagree. Hand these to your writer as the canonical text.

primitive.event

Event

An immutable record of something that happened. Proposed events come from one side; bilateral (mutual-authorization) events require confirmation from both parties.

Constraint: Append-only. No updates. No deletes. Ever.

e.g. invoice_ingested → vendor_confirmed → posting_generated
primitive.policy

Policy Version

A versioned, immutable rule set, pinned to every event at the time it is processed. It governs triggers, auto-approve conditions, and how exceptions are handled.

Constraint: Policies cannot be changed retroactively. Earlier events keep the policy version that applied when they occurred.

e.g. POL-v4.2 — first-payment, bank-change, >$10K triggers + 7 auto-approve conditions
primitive.work_order

Work Order

The container that holds the full context and decisions for a payment. It tracks three parallel states — Authorization, Control, and Settlement — without collapsing them.

Constraint: Every decision is recorded as an immutable event.

three tracks: authorization_state · control_state · settlement_state
primitive.posting

Posting

A journal-ready ledger entry, created only after mutual authorization is complete. It carries the full history back to the original event.

Constraint: A posting cannot exist without a completed mutual-authorization record (non-nullable FK to the bilateral event).

"Ledger" is the layer; Posting is the object the brief mislabeled.
primitive.case

Case

A governed exception — a dispute, non-response, budget breach, or policy gap. Resolution events are appended, but prior events are never changed or deleted.

Constraint: The record of the exception is permanent.

this is the fifth primitive the brief dropped in favor of "Capacity."

Lineage (a property)
The primitives / Lineage

Lineage is the proof — and it's a property, not an object.

Your live site renders it as lineage() with the caption "the proof is the asset." It is the immutable hash chain that links a Posting back through its events, decisions, and policy version. Model it as a function over the five objects — never as a sixth box beside them.

verify.ts — independent replay
// Any third party, no counterparty contact
const ok = await replay(eventId);

// Standard libs only — no proprietary verifier
- SHA256(canonicalize(payload, v1))
- ECDSA-P256 signature check
- X.509 chain validation

return {
  valid: ok,
  proves: "both parties confirmed",
  at: "signed timestamp",
  unmodified_since: true
};
Why this matters

The subscription fee is the cost of producing this verifiable record. Lineage is what makes a transaction insurable, financeable, and survivable in audit — which is the entire economic case.

Deterministic commerce
Foundations / Deterministic commerce

Why reconciliation should not exist.

The brief's strongest foundation topic, and it's correct as written. When both parties co-author the same event, there are no two independent records to reconcile — agreement is guaranteed by construction.

  • Why reconciliation exists: each party keeps a private record of the same transaction.
  • Why it's waste: when records disagree, both sides reconstruct from email and PDFs — slow, costly, inconclusive.
  • Event-sourced finance: the append-only log is the system of record; everything else is a projection.
  • Deterministic settlement: the same events produce identical outputs for all parties.

Zero-trust operations
Foundations / Zero-trust operations

Trust is not a control.

Network security abandoned the trusted perimeter a decade ago. Commercial payments never did. CAST is zero-trust applied to the movement of value: no covered payment is trusted because of who submitted it — only after the counterparty independently co-authors the terms.

  • Identity-bound action: WebAuthn ties each confirmation to a hardware-backed key, not an inbox.
  • Least-privilege verification: the vendor sees only their own record; buyer GL codes and budgets are never exposed.
  • Continuous validation: a bank-account change re-triggers the gate. Trust is re-established at every covered event.
  • Separation of decision and execution: the actor that proposes is never the sole authority that releases.

Liability as a service
Foundations / Liability as a service

Who is responsible when AI acts?

When AI commoditizes execution, value moves to whoever can verifiably underwrite the output and accept liability for it. The Work Order decision record is the proof of underwriting — and the Catalini framework calls this Liability-as-a-Service.

The AI Sandwich

Human intent sets the policy → machine execution generates output → human verification closes the loop with an approval that carries personal accountability. The Work Order is where the sandwich is made concrete.

Promote the Catalini, Hui & Wu "Some Simple Economics of AGI" (2026) paper from the brief's reading list into a first-class thesis. It's the macroeconomic why behind the whole company; filing it under "external research" undersells it.

Agentic commerce
Foundations / Agentic commerce

Agentic commerce needs settlement infrastructure.

When agents transact, the bilateral question gets more urgent, not less: there must be a signed record both principals hold and any third party can verify. CAST already requires every event to declare its actor mode — human, agent, system, or counterparty — and to name the accountable human owner whenever an action can change financial state.

The line that anchors the section

An agent can be the performer. A human must remain the accountable owner. The decision record keeps the two distinct — permanently, and verifiably.
