The thesis

Every attack in the dossier exploits the same structural flaw: a payment process where one party authorizes and everyone else trusts. The vendor — the only party who can confirm whether a request is legitimate — is absent from the authorization moment.

CAST closes that gap by making the bilateral event the authorization. No event, no payment. The Trade Message travels to a passkey device controlled by the registered vendor signer; the inbox is not the confirmation channel. Bank account changes and rush payments are hard-coded triggers, not policy-configurable.

The result is reproducible on each of the eleven cases below. We make no claim against fraud arising from compromised onboarding or insider collusion — those require upstream identity controls — and we mark such cases honestly.

The claim, stated precisely

CAST eliminates the class of fraud that depends on one party acting without the other party's knowledge. Ten of these eleven attacks fit that class exactly. The eleventh — fake-vendor onboarding — is mitigated by CAST's provisional tier and made detectable, but is not guaranteed: it sits in the residual surface of physical device compromise, weak onboarding, and insider collusion that no payment-confirmation system can eliminate alone.
