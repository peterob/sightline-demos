How to use this matrix.
  1. HAVE (18): Wire these into the relevant tool in Sprint 1–2. The blocker is plumbing, not data collection. Highest near-term ROI because the measurement work is already done.
  2. AVAILABLE (12): Work with the system owner (RCM vendor, EHR admin, HR) to configure or extract. Most can be closed in a single working session per input — these become sprint-level tasks, not net-new tool builds.
  3. TO BUILD / SOURCE (8): These are the real prioritization conversation. Rank by FW impact: CareFirst avoided cost, denial overturn rate, and documentation completeness score are the three highest-leverage gaps. Physician feedback pulse, VBC inventory, and Epic/Redox partner status are operationally cheap adds. Days-to-first-session onboarding and full downstream utilization data require cross-system work or payer data access.
Strategic read: Culina already has most of what it needs — the constraint is wiring and reporting discipline, not collection. The 8 "to build" inputs concentrate on the FW3 payer loop, which is also the one the Series B narrative depends on most. Sequence the build accordingly.
