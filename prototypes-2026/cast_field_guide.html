The ProblemCAST
01 · Where value leaks

A unilateral instruction with a hope attached.

Every commercial payment today is authorized by one side. The payer approves an invoice internally; the counterparty learns of the transaction only when — or if — the money arrives.

Digital tools removed the physical constraints of commerce — the checks, the branches, the paper. They left the underlying trust model untouched. A payment process where only one of two parties has confirmed the terms before value transfers is not a bilateral authorization. It is a unilateral instruction with a hope attached.

$2.9B
Annual U.S. losses to Business Email Compromise — fraud inserted into a process never designed to verify the counterparty.
Days→min
The reconciliation drag that disappears when both parties co-author one record.

What the gap costs

  • Fraud. A fraudulent instruction — a changed bank account, a first payment to a new vendor — slips through because nothing checks the other party before release.
  • Reconciliation. Two private records of one transaction. When they disagree, both sides reconstruct from email and PDFs.
  • Audit and lending friction. Every downstream party — auditor, lender, insurer — verifies independently, expensively, and often inconclusively.
The root cause is structural

This is not a software bug to patch. The trust model under commercial payments was never bilateral. CAST adds one narrow control where it is missing.

The ReframeCAST
02 · Verification is the invariant

Not a payments bug. A verification gap.

Stopping payment fraud is the door most people walk through. Behind it is a larger shift — one that reframes what CAST actually is.

As AI commoditizes execution — the cost of generating financial output approaches zero — the binding constraint in the economy moves from production to verification. The question stops being can we produce this output and becomes can we prove this output is trustworthy.

CAST expands the verifiable share of economic activity by replacing opinion-based reconstruction — an auditor reading email threads — with proof-based verification: a lineage-hash check anyone can run, without contacting either counterparty.

The economic foundation

This is the verification-bottleneck thesis (Catalini, Hui & Wu, 2026): in a world of cheap output, the scarce and valuable thing is provable trust. CAST produces it.

The shift, in one line

FROM

Systems of record

Each party keeps a private copy. Truth is reconstructed after the fact. Reconciliation is a permanent cost.

TO

Systems of coordination

One co-authored record. Truth is established at the moment of agreement. Reconciliation is structurally unnecessary.

What CAST IsCAST
03 · The bilateral gate

One record, co-authored, before value moves.

Before a covered payment is released, the counterparty confirms the details through a channel cryptographically bound to a verified identity. That co-authored record becomes the authorization.

The confirmation is signed, timestamped, pinned to the policy version in force, and tamper-evident. It travels with the payment through the ERP, the audit, the lender, and the insurer — as proof none of them has to reconstruct.

What CAST is not

  • Not an ERP replacement. It sits alongside your systems and exports posting-ready journals with the lineage hash attached.
  • Not a fraud guarantee. It eliminates unilateral fraud; collusion becomes documented and detectable, not impossible.
  • Not a point solution. Accounts payable is the first instantiation of a vertical-agnostic architecture.

The ArchitectureCAST
04 · Five primitives

Everything else is a projection of these five objects.

CAST is built on five canonical objects. They do not change between verticals — only the event types, policy rules, and work-order structures do.

primitive.event

Event

An immutable record of something that happened. Append-only — no updates, no deletes, ever. The only source of truth.

primitive.policy

Policy Version

A versioned, immutable rule set, pinned to every event at processing time. Never changed retroactively.

primitive.work_order

Work Order

The container holding context and decisions, tracking authorization, control, and settlement in parallel.

primitive.posting

Posting

A journal-ready ledger entry, created only after mutual authorization. Carries its full history.

primitive.case

Case

A governed exception — dispute, non-response, breach. Resolution events append; nothing prior is rewritten.

lineage()

Lineage — a property

The immutable hash chain binding every posting back to its originating event. Not a sixth object — the thread through all five.

Why this matters

Lineage is what makes a transaction insurable, financeable, and survivable in audit. The proof is the asset.

The ArchitectureCAST
05 · Seven structural invariants

Impossible, not merely tedious.

A useful test for any control: does it make the failure impossible, or just tedious? Controls that only add friction degrade against a determined adversary. The ones that hold remove the capability outright.

CAST's seven invariants pass that test. They are enforced at the schema level — through foreign keys, triggers, and unique indexes — so they hold against a bug, a migration, or a direct database write. They are structural impossibilities, not policies the application is trusted to follow.

1A Posting requires a Bilateral Event — non-nullable foreign key.
2A Bilateral Event requires a confirmed Work Order state — authorization precedes the record.
3Lineage hash chain integrity across all events — a break is detectable.
4A single active Policy Version at any time — enforced by a partial unique index.
5Settlement export requires an approved Control track — the decision precedes the disbursement.
6Hard-coded triggers cannot be policy-suppressed — first payment, bank-account change.
7Events are append-only — no updates, no deletes. A correction is a new event.
Why at the schema level

Application code changes weekly; the schema survives every refactor. A verifier reading the database in 2031 can confirm all seven held — because the constraints are part of the data model itself.

The NetworkCAST
07 · The Vendor Event Layer

Proof the counterparty owns — and carries.

When a vendor confirms a payment, the buyer's system records it permanently. The vendor gets a screen that disappears when they close the browser.

The gap

If a dispute arises ninety days later, the vendor's evidence is: "I got a message and tapped a button." That is not a proof record. It is a memory.

The Vendor Event Layer gives every vendor a persistent, exportable record of every bilateral event they have co-authored — across all of their buyers — that they own and can share with any auditor, lender, or insurer. It is a vendor-scoped projection of events that already exist; it creates no second ledger.

Why it compounds

A vendor who has confirmed 200 transactions across 12 buyers has built a trust history — an asset they carry, not data trapped on each buyer's side. Every confirmation thickens that history, which makes them easier for the next buyer to trust, the next lender to underwrite, the next auditor to clear.

Validation as a service

A signed proof export lets a third party verify a specific bilateral event without contacting CAST or the buyer. The proof travels with the vendor — across the whole network, from events created anyway.

The HorizonCAST
08 · Agentic commerce

The settlement layer for an agentic economy.

As autonomous agents begin to initiate payments, negotiate terms, and settle obligations, the bilateral question gets more urgent — not less.

Zero-trust frameworks for AI agents focus on keeping an agent from being hijacked. They stop at the boundary of the transaction. When an agent legitimately initiates a payment, a well-secured agent is still not proof the counterparty agreed. CAST is the layer below: even a perfectly-secured agent's payment requires bilateral co-authorship before value moves.

Attribution is the precondition

Every event declares its actor mode — human, agent, system, or counterparty — and names the accountable human owner whenever an action can change financial state. An agent can be the performer; a human remains accountable. The record proves which was which.

Why this strengthens the case

The agentic future does not weaken the argument for bilateral proof — it is the argument. As execution gets cheaper and faster, the only durable asset is the ability to prove, years later, that an event occurred and what it contained. The more autonomous the actors, the more that proof is worth.
