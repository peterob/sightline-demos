AP Workflow — End to End
Same scenario. Same vendors. Same people. Fundamentally different process.
WITHOUT CAST
Manual · Fragmented · Reconstructed after the fact
👤 4–6 humans ⚙ 6–7 systems ⚠ No proof chain
STEP 1
Invoice arrives
Email to AP inbox, or uploaded to AP tool, or EDI from vendor system. AP clerk opens and manually reviews.
👤 AP Clerk 📧 Email / AP tool
No record of who reviewed it, when, or what they checked. If they miss a duplicate, it goes through.
STEP 2
Manual data entry into ERP
AP clerk re-keys invoice details into NetSuite or QuickBooks. Matches to PO manually. Flags discrepancies by email.
👤 AP Clerk ⚙ ERP
Data entry errors. No automatic PO matching. Duplicate invoices caught only if clerk remembers.
STEP 3
Email approval routing
AP clerk emails manager for approval. Manager emails CFO for large amounts. Approval lives in someone's inbox.
👤 AP Manager 👤 CFO 📧 Email
Approval chain is an email thread. If person leaves, audit trail is gone. No SLA enforcement. No structured record of what was approved and why.
STEP 4
Vendor verification — by email
AP clerk emails vendor to confirm bank details. Vendor replies by email. No structural check on changed accounts.
👤 AP Clerk 🏢 Vendor contact 📧 Email
⚠ Primary BEC attack vector. A spoofed email changing the bank account bypasses all controls. Wire fraud typically happens here.
STEP 5
Payment initiated in bank portal
AP clerk logs into bank portal, manually enters wire or ACH details. No automated link to the invoice or approval that authorized it.
👤 AP Clerk 🏦 Bank portal ⚡ ACH / Wire
Bank portal has no record of the invoice. ERP has no record of the bank instruction. No causal link exists between the two systems.
STEP 6
Month-end reconciliation
Controller pulls bank statements, matches to ERP entries, investigates gaps. AP clerk tracks exceptions in a spreadsheet.
👤 Controller 📊 Spreadsheet ⚙ ERP
Takes 3–5 days. Exceptions tracked in Excel with no audit trail. Errors found weeks after they occurred.
STEP 7
Audit — reconstruction
Auditor asks for proof of decisions. AP team spends 1–3 weeks pulling email chains, ERP logs, bank statements, and spreadsheets into a manual package.
👤 AP Manager 👤 Controller ⚠ 4 systems
Evidence is assembled after the fact. Not proof — a story. Auditors raise findings because the chain cannot be verified independently.
VS
WITH CAST
Agent-governed · Proof at execution · Permanently traceable
🤖 1 AI agent 👤 2 humans (exceptions only) ⛓ ProofChain on every event
STEP 1
Invoice received → ProposedEvent
Email parser reads inbound invoice. CAST creates a ProposedEvent — signed, timestamped, with agent identity and policy version. This is the root of the ProofChain.
🤖 cast-ap-agent-v2.1 📧 Email parser ⛓ EVT-001 · hash: a3f9...
Duplicate detection is structural. Every invoice gets a unique event ID. Duplicates are flagged before any human sees them.
STEP 2
InvariantCore check — automatic
Policy engine checks all 6 InvariantCore rules in under 1 second. Agent identity, amount threshold, vendor status, PO match, bank account change detection. Pass or fail — no manual judgment.
🤖 Policy engine ⛓ EVT-002 · INV-CORE-v14
Rules enforced before execution — not logged after. Every rule check is permanently recorded with the policy version that governed it.
STEP 3
Trade Message sent — automatically
Agent sends structured Trade Message to vendor via email and mobile web. Invoice ref, amount, date, account ending, PO ref, reply deadline. Three buttons: Confirm · Request Change · Dispute.
🤖 cast-ap-agent-v2.1 🏢 Vendor portal ⛓ EVT-003 · hash: c2e8...
Replaces email back-and-forth. Bank account change triggers a mandatory fresh Trade Message — BEC attack surface eliminated structurally.
STEP 3b
If rules fail → DisputeEvent
Payment blocked immediately. DisputeEvent created with reason codes. Work Order routed to Sarah Chen with SLA timer. CFO dual-attestation Work Order queued if over $50K.
🚫 Payment blocked 👤 Sarah Chen 👤 CFO ⛓ EXC-019 → VENDOR-KYC-v2
STEP 4
AcknowledgeEvent — bilateral signed
Vendor taps Confirm on mobile. Both parties have now signed. Bilateral AcknowledgeEvent created — structurally different from a unilateral system log. ProofChain advances.
🏢 J. Torres, BrightPath ⛓ EVT-004 · bilateral: true
Vendor signature is in the ProofChain. It cannot be removed. The auditor sees who confirmed, when, and from which vendor contact.
STEP 5
LedgerPosting + settlement
Double-entry posting created. proof_chain_id attached — non-nullable at DB layer. Payment routed to settlement rail. ACH (2-day fiat) or USDC on Base (sub-second stablecoin).
🤖 GL engine ⚡ ACH ⚡ USDC on Base ⛓ PC-INV1048-001
Ledger posting is structurally impossible without a ProofChain. The database physically rejects any posting that lacks lineage. No workaround exists.
STEP 6
Reconciliation — continuous
No month-end reconciliation step. Every posting has its ProofChain attached at creation. GL is always reconciled because every entry already has lineage.
🤖 Automated ⛓ Continuous
Replaces 3–5 days of month-end reconciliation. Controller reviews exceptions only — not routine entries.
STEP 7
Audit — proof generated at execution
Auditor requests AttestView. AUDIT-EXPORT-v1 policy generates a scoped, signed export in seconds. Event type, amounts, agent identity, policy version, proof hashes — independently verifiable. Sensitive fields automatically redacted at data model layer.
🤖 Attest engine ⛓ Signed · scoped · seconds
Not a reconstruction. Not a story. A proof chain generated at execution time, independently verifiable, every decision causally linked from the first ProposedEvent to the final ledger posting.
