01 Build now
design requirements, not questions
Three-track state model
Architecture
Bilateral, control, and settlement as parallel tracks — not a single status field. This must be correct in the data model before writing the first screen.
Retrofitting parallel tracks after launch is expensive. A collapsed status field can't answer "confirmed by vendor but blocked by policy?" — which is exactly the state that matters most.
Immutable policy versioning
Data Architecture
Every bilateral event pins the exact policy version in effect at the time of approval. A policy change later cannot retroactively alter what was approved. This is a data architecture requirement, not a UI feature.
If policy versioning is wrong from day one, the FCA defense story breaks and the audit trail is unreliable. No retrofit path exists — the write is already immutable or it isn't.
Non-response fallback design
Core Flow
The spec is silent on what happens when a vendor doesn't respond by deadline. Design this before launch. Proposed path: second attempt, then escalate to mandatory internal approval with override reason appended to the event record.
Blocking payment indefinitely causes the product to be bypassed. But uncontrolled non-response weakens the bilateral proof claim. The override-with-documentation path preserves both properties.
Vendor bank-change dispute sketch
Exception Flow
The highest-stakes scenario: a legitimate vendor flags a fraudulent bank-change on a trade message. Doesn't need to be fully built in v1, but needs a defined path — who is notified, what is the resolution sequence, how is the case closed.
No enterprise buyer will sign off without an answer to this. This is the scenario the product was built to prevent. Leaving it undefined in the spec is a sales blocker.
02 Decide before building
commitments with downstream consequences
ERP integration target
Product Scope
Pick one for v1. NetSuite and Sage Intacct cover most of the PE portfolio beachhead. QuickBooks covers the smaller end. "Manual export only" is a legitimate choice but must be explicit in early sales conversations.
This shapes the export schema, field mapping, and implementation estimate. It also determines whether the first pilot company can actually close the loop — or leaves the AP analyst doing manual data entry.
Sales channel commitment
Go-to-Market
PE operating partners, DFHQ fractional engagements, or direct to portfolio company CFOs — these are different products. A fractional CFO deploying across a portfolio needs multi-entity management. A solo controller deploying at one company doesn't.
The admin/vendor trust setup screen design depends on this answer. Design for the wrong deployer and implementation friction kills the first few deals.
Pricing model structure
Commercial
Per-message pricing creates the wrong incentive — AP teams will suppress triggers to reduce cost. Platform fee per company with coverage metrics as the value story is probably right. Structure must be set before the first sales conversation.
Pricing structure shapes adoption behavior. The wrong model directly undermines the product's stated goal of covering high-risk AP flows as broadly as possible.
03 Validate with buyers first
assumptions, not facts
Vendor response rate
Load-bearing assumption
The entire bilateral value prop rests on vendors actually responding. Run a manual test before building the automated flow: take 20 real invoices from a friendly portfolio company, send structured confirmation requests by email, measure response rate and friction.
The answer may be high for large recurring vendors and low for small or one-time vendors — which immediately sharpens the trigger policy and scope for v1.
Magic link / OTP as sufficient proof
Proof Standard
Ask two or three serious buyers directly: "If a vendor confirms via email link and OTP, is that sufficient proof for your audit trail and FCA defense?" Some will say yes. Some will require something stronger. The answer determines whether passkeys are a roadmap feature or a v1 requirement.
An attacker who controls the vendor's email can confirm a fraudulent trade message via magic link. The cryptographic strength of v1 confirmation is directly tied to the defensibility of the bilateral proof claim.
What "sufficient proof" means to an auditor
Enterprise Sales Blocker
Show the event log concept — CAST reference, bilateral confirmation timestamp, policy version, signer identity — to one or two nonprofit compliance officers or grant auditors. Ask whether this record would satisfy their audit evidence requirements.
If yes, the FCA defense story holds as the enterprise sales anchor. If they want something additional, you learn that before building the wrong audit trail UI.
