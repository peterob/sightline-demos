Flywheel A

Clinical outcomes quality

The production function. High-quality documented outcomes feed every other loop — they are the raw material of the business.

Drivers

  • RD documentation quality — CPT + biometric delta + rationale
  • Note turnaround SLA <24hrs post-session
  • Episode completion rate — patients reaching 4+ sessions
  • Biometric state-change capture — A1c, weight, lipids
  • Clinical protocol adherence (Culina Health Method)

Outputs that feed other loops

  • → FW-B: Documented outcomes make the return-referral stage possible
  • → FW-D: Aggregated state-change data becomes the payer waste-reduction case
  • → FW-C: Complete episodes = billable sessions = revenue per RD
Why separated from the old FW1: The prior framework merged "clinical outcomes" with "referral trust" into one loop. The JD treats outcomes as an input to the referral loop, not the loop itself. Separating makes each measurable on its own terms.
Flywheel B · New

Referral network loop

"Model and evaluate the end-to-end referral network: physician-to-patient, treatment outcomes, and return loop. Quantify conversion rates, revenue per referred patient, and the financial case for network investment." — JD, verbatim.
STAGE 1 · OUT
Physician → patient
Conversion: referral sent → scheduled
STAGE 2 · THROUGH
Treatment & outcomes
Conversion: scheduled → episode complete
STAGE 3 · BACK
Return to physician
Conversion: note returned → physician engaged
STAGE 4 · COMPOUND
Next referral
Conversion: engaged → second referral sent

Top-level KPIs

  • Revenue per referred patient — LTV × conversion × sessions/episode × rate
  • Network activation rate — % of physician groups active
  • Network payback period — months to recover BD / integration cost
  • Re-referral rate — % of groups sending ≥2nd referral

Break points

  • Stage 1 fails: no Epic/Redox integration — referral never arrives structured
  • Stage 2 fails: episode <2 sessions — nothing to report
  • Stage 3 fails: note returned but not read — physician never sees outcome
  • Stage 4 fails: positive outcome, no follow-up referral — loop doesn't compound
Why a dedicated loop: The JD names this explicitly as one modeled object with stage-by-stage conversion and a named financial output. The old framework split it across two loops, with Stage 3 (the "Back") reduced to an implicit assumption. This elevation makes the full funnel measurable — and gives the role a single model to own.
Flywheel C · Retained

Utilization → margin → reinvestment

Filling RD capacity converts fixed cost into contribution margin, which funds the next cohort without dilution. Unchanged from prior framework — operationally sound.

Drivers

  • Fill rate (target >80%)
  • No-show rate (target <8%)
  • Net effective sessions/RD/week (target 17–18)
  • PA first-pass approval (87% → 90%)
  • Clean claim rate & AR aging
  • Denial overturn yield ($ recovered ÷ $ appealed)

Outputs

  • Revenue per RD crosses $112.5k loaded cost
  • Contribution margin per pod
  • Working capital — faster cash, lower AR
  • Funds next RD cohort & infrastructure investment
What changed: "Reinvestment" is now explicit in the loop name — it's how Finance thinks about this (capital allocation), which matters for the JD's FP&A lens. Denial overturn yield added as a named driver.
Flywheel D · Narrowed

Payer value → scale

Proving downstream cost avoidance to CareFirst (and future payers) in their language unlocks member volume and contract expansion. Now narrowly focused on the payer-economics loop.

Drivers

  • Avoided ER / hospitalization cost per managed member
  • A1c / weight / lipid delta aggregated to population
  • UM denial rate by reason code
  • SDOH / equity composition (55%+ patients of color)
  • Deterministic attestation quality — non-subjective documentation

Outputs

  • Avoided cost / member exceeds $7k — contract leverage
  • CareFirst exclusive status expands to more products
  • VBC / PMPM contract optionality
  • Series B narrative and strategic buyer optionality
What changed: Removed "outcomes as referral trust" — that now lives in FW-A & FW-B. This loop is now strictly the payer-economics case. Narrower, sharper, and cleanly owned.

Architecture — loops vs. meta-layers

Flywheels are the operating loops the business runs on. The JD's other major responsibility areas are not loops — they sit above the loops as consolidating layers or across them as integrators. Keeping them separated from the flywheels prevents the framework from bloating every loop with cross-cutting concerns.

Consolidating layer

Strategic Finance & FP&A

Sits above all four flywheels. Consolidates their outputs into the P&L, forecast, scenarios, and board pack. Tools: C1–C5.

Meta layer

Cross-functional cadence

Sits across all four flywheels. Weekly scorecard health, risk surfacing, dependency tracking. Tools: B1, B2.

Operational surface

Partnership Operations

Executes where FW-B, FW-C, and FW-D intersect. Readiness, billing compliance, rev share. Tools: A1, A2, A3.

Changes from v1 (three-loop framework)
Split: Old FW1 (Clinical outcomes → Referral trust) broke into FW-A (Clinical outcomes quality) and FW-B (Referral network loop). Outcomes are now an input to the referral loop, not co-mingled with it.
Elevated: The Referral Network Loop is now first-class with four explicit stages (Out, Through, Back, Compound) and revenue per referred patient as a named top-level KPI. This directly implements the JD's explicit call-out.
Narrowed: FW-D (Payer value → scale) lost the "outcomes build referral trust" meaning it previously carried — that's now FW-A and FW-B. FW-D is strictly the CareFirst-style waste-reduction economics loop.
Retained: FW-C (Utilization → margin → reinvestment) is structurally unchanged. Only added denial overturn yield as a named driver and renamed output to emphasize the reinvestment mechanism.
Clarified: Strategic Finance, Cross-functional cadence, and Partnership Ops are now explicit as meta-layers, not loops — matching how the JD actually describes them.
