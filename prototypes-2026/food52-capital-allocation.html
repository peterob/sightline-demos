Root Cause · The $1 Test Failed Twice

The 2021 acquisitions ($48M Schoolhouse + Dansk) converted a high-margin content-and-commerce model into an asset-heavy manufacturing business — without clearing the hurdle rate. Incremental capital piled into fixed infrastructure while revenue declined, creating a fixed-cost trap that cash flows could never escape.

THE STRUCTURAL BREAKDOWN
Content-to-commerce worked when the cost to acquire an audience was borne by editorial content — and conversion was organic. The Schoolhouse acquisition destroyed this by adding $44.7M in asset-heavy, low-margin revenue that overwhelmed the high-margin advertising engine.
WHAT ROIIC WOULD HAVE SHOWN
$48M deployed into Schoolhouse at a <5% ROIIC vs. a 15% hurdle rate destroys ~$490M in NPV over 10 years (assumes 10% WACC). Incremental capital into the ad studio at 28% ROIIC would have created >$150M in NPV from the same base. The math was available. It was not applied.
WHAT ATK IS BUYING FOR $6.5M
The surviving asset: 37M monthly reach, in-house ad studio, community platform, and editorial brand — the original high-ROIIC core. The manufacturing assets (Schoolhouse factory, Dansk inventory) are the write-off. ATK effectively acquires the content flywheel at near-zero cost after the acquisition strategy incinerated the balance sheet.
