Coverage summary & gaps by flywheel

Flywheel 1 — Outcomes → Referral Trust

10/17
tools produce FW1 inputs
Fully instrumented
Note SLA, episode completion, referral-to-first-session, activation %, re-engagement, CAC-by-channel — all have a named owner and weekly or monthly cadence.
Gap to close
Physician-side feedback signal is inferred from referral volume, not measured directly. Consider adding a quarterly physician NPS or "note usefulness" pulse to B3 inputs.

Flywheel 2 — Utilization → Margin

13/17
tools produce FW2 inputs
Fully instrumented
This is the most densely measured loop. Every driver (fill rate, no-shows, loaded cost, PA approval, clean claim rate, cash conversion, next-cohort trigger) has a direct tool output.
Gap to close
Denial-recovery yield (appealed vs. recovered $) is implied but not broken out. Add a line to A2 or D5 — it's the single biggest hidden FW2 lever.

Flywheel 3 — Payer Value → Scale

8/17
tools produce FW3 inputs
Fully instrumented
A3 is the primary instrument — avoided cost, A1c delta, UM denial by reason, ROI ratio. D5 captures the deterministic documentation that feeds A3. C5 surfaces it for the board.
Gap to close
SDOH / equity data (55%+ patients of color) is a FW3 driver but not explicitly in any tool. Add as a line in A3 — it's cheap to track and CareFirst values it.
Read of the map. Every flywheel driver named in the framework traces to at least one tool in the sprint, and most trace to two or three — giving triangulation rather than a single point of failure. FW2 is overbuilt by design: it's the nearest-term financial survival loop and the one the board will read first. FW1 is well-covered on leading indicators (note SLA, activation rate) through D1/D5/B3. FW3 has the thinnest coverage — deliberately, because A3 alone compresses most of the drivers into the quarterly payer-language report, but this is also where adding one equity metric and one denial-reason-code drill-down would close the remaining gap. Three weekly leading indicators — note return SLA (D5), PA approval (A2/D5), utilization (D1) — are the early-warning system for all three flywheels, which is why the command pack (B1) surfaces them to the CEO every Monday.
