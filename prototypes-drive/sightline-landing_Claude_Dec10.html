Core Primitive

What Is the
Causal Tensor?

A Causal Tensor is Sightline's atomic primitive. Each business event is captured as a self-contained, replayable state transition that encodes what actually happened—not what someone typed into a ledger.

Tensor cells are chained together into a DAG of events that represents your business reality with mathematical precision. Any point-in-time view is just a deterministic query over this graph.

01

Observation

What the system believed to be true—inputs, balances, quantities, locations. The ground truth at the moment of the event.

02

Operation

The attempted change—approve invoice, ship goods, release payment. Every mutation is explicit and traceable.

03

Obligation / Proof

The policies, invariants, and evidence required for acceptance. Every transition carries its justification.

Deep Dive

Architecture Overview

Sightline's architecture intentionally separates fact capture, policy evaluation, and explainability into distinct, composable layers.

Layer 01

Causal Tensor Layer

  • Event DAG storage
  • State snapshots and deltas
  • Idempotent ingestion
  • Deterministic replay
Layer 02

Policy Engine Layer

  • Declarative invariants
  • Quantity, pricing, timing rules
  • Versioned policies with tests
  • Outcome classification
Layer 03

Proof Layer

  • Human-readable explanations
  • Audit trails and narratives
  • Investigation views
  • API hooks for ERP / FP&A

"If blockchain gives you consensus over who owns a coin, Causal Tensor gives you consensus over what actually happened in your business."
