The Problem

Garbage in, garbage out.
You know this.

Traditional financial systems treat all data equally. But some data is verified by independent systems with skin in the game. Other data is just... assertions. Sightline knows the difference.

Data Trust Framework

Not all data is created equal

We classify every input by trustworthiness. High-trust data powers automation. Low-trust data requires verification.

High Trust

Automate with confidence

Data from systems with independent accountability. Their business depends on accuracy.

  • Payment confirmed (Stripe, Shopify Payments)
  • Order fulfilled (Shopify + Carrier tracking)
  • Funds deposited (Bank confirmation)
  • Refund issued (Payment processor)
Medium Trust

Verify when possible

Data from counterparties with aligned but not identical incentives. Cross-reference before acting.

  • PO issued (Borrower ERP)
  • Goods shipped (Supplier assertion)
  • Invoice amount (Supplier)
  • Product pricing (Catalog data)
Low Trust

Require independent verification

Data the counterparty controls and has incentive to misrepresent. Never automate without verification.

  • Goods received (Borrower-asserted)
  • Inventory count (Borrower WMS)
  • Cost basis (Borrower-reported)
  • Sales forecast (Borrower opinion)
