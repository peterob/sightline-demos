How Policy Binds to the Transaction

📊 Limits: Amount caps, frequency rules, conditions

What Lives Inside the Policy Reference

📜 Policy Identity

  • policy_id Unique identifier
  • policy_type Contract, Budget, Standing Order, etc.
  • policy_hash Cryptographic proof of terms
  • effective_period Valid from/to dates

👥 Authorization Chain

  • counterparties[] All parties to the agreement
  • signatories[] Who signed / approved
  • delegations[] Authority passed to whom
  • execution_role Who is executing this tx

📏 Constraints (WF Rules)

  • allowed_actions[] What action types permitted
  • allowed_directions[] What account flows permitted
  • amount_limits{} Min/max per tx, per period
  • conditions[] Prerequisites that must be true

📎 Linkages

  • parent_policy What this derives from
  • related_events[] Prior events under this policy
  • remaining_capacity Budget/quantity remaining
  • attestations[] Third-party confirmations

Why Policy Binding Changes Everything

🔍 Self-Explaining Variances

When cash is off-forecast, you don't just see "Purchases exceeded budget." You see "PO-2024-0847 authorized $3M for GPU procurement; $847K executed against remaining capacity of $2.15M."

🤝 Multi-Party Proof

In GPU procurement across buyer, seller, and financing partner—each party sees the same event, bound to the same policy they all signed. No reconciliation needed; the proof is in the structure.

🚫 Invalid Events Impossible

A transaction without a valid policy reference is structurally malformed. You can't record "Debit: Expenses, Credit: Cash" without answering "under what authority?" first.

📊 Budget = Policy Consumption

Budget variance isn't calculated—it's observed. Each transaction decrements its policy's remaining capacity. Real-time burn rate with full causal chain.

🔗 Audit Trail by Design

Every transaction links to its authorizing policy, which links to its parent budget, which links to board approval. The trail isn't reconstructed; it's intrinsic.

🤖 AI-Ready Governance

An AI agent can execute transactions if given policy delegation. The policy constrains what actions it can take. Autonomous operations with human-defined guardrails.
