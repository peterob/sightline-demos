The Problem

Every business involving more than one party faces the same challenge: they don't share a source of truth. Each party maintains their own system, exports data, sends it to counterparties, and hopes it matches. When it doesn't—and it always doesn't—they reconcile.

Reconciliation isn't a bug. It's the architecture. When two parties maintain separate systems, disagreement is inevitable. Reconciliation is just discovering how much you disagree and negotiating a settlement.

High-Frequency Transactions

Become uneconomical because reconciliation cost exceeds transaction value.

Multi-Party Arrangements

Become exponentially complex as each party adds their own system.

Real-Time Visibility

Impossible because "truth" doesn't exist until reconciliation completes.

Automation

Blocked because you can't automate decisions on data you don't trust.

Why Current Solutions Fail

Solution Why It's Inadequate
ERP Systems Single-party by design. NetSuite can't give a lender real-time access to a borrower's inventory. They receive a report—stale before it arrives.
APIs & Integrations Move data, not truth. A copy can drift, be misinterpreted, have different rules applied. Information moves, but agreement doesn't.
Reconciliation Software Optimizes the symptom, not the cause. Building a faster ambulance instead of preventing the accident.
Blockchain Right intuition, wrong abstraction. Consensus for adversarial environments. Smart contracts can't be amended. Ignores that disputes need human resolution.

The Primitives

Seven primitives necessary and sufficient for any governed financial operation. Everything else—invoices, POs, loans, payouts—is a projection over these.

1

Event

What happened? Immutable fact from a trusted source.

2

PolicyVersion

How to interpret it? Versioned, deterministic rules.

3

Obligation

What is owed? Commitment between parties.

4

Allocation

How decomposed? Line items, splits, tags.

5

Settlement

How fulfilled? Partial or complete satisfaction.

6

Posting

How recorded? Double-entry ledger.

7

Case

What needs resolution? Exceptions, disputes.

Data Trust

Shared computation only works with trusted inputs. We classify every source:

Trust Criteria Action
High Source has independent accountability (Shopify, 3PL, Bank) Automate
Medium Aligned but not identical incentives (Supplier, ERP) Verify
Low Incentive to misrepresent (Borrower-reported) Require proof

The 3PL as trust anchor: When inventory ships to a 3PL and orders fulfill from that 3PL, the borrower never touches the goods. "Borrower-reported inventory" becomes "3PL-verified inventory."

What This Enables

Products that weren't viable. PO-level inventory financing—each purchase order financed as an isolated silo with auto-repayment—was always theoretically possible. Reconciliation cost made it uneconomical. Now it works.

Real-time visibility. A lender doesn't wait for monthly reports. They see every sale as it happens (Shopify), every inventory movement (3PL). The position is always current because it's computed, not reported.

Risk isolation. When each PO is a separate silo, a slow SKU doesn't contaminate the portfolio. Exposure is granular. Performing inventory isn't hostage to one bad product.

Audit as replay. When state derives from events + policy, audit is trivial: replay the computation. Verify by running the same function on the same events.
