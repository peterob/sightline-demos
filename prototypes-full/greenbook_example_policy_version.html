GreenBook §9.05–9.11 — Change Assessment Process

Which Policy Was In Effect?

The GreenBook requires documenting change assessment processes so "the internal control system can be quickly adapted." During emergencies, policies change rapidly. Auditors need to know: which rules applied when this payment was made?
Scenario: Emergency Small Business Grant
Congress authorizes emergency relief. The agency issues guidance, then revises it twice in 30 days as implementation problems emerge. Eligibility criteria change with each version.

Question: A business received $50,000 on April 5. Was it eligible under the rules that existed on April 5—or are we judging it by rules that came later?

Effective: March 15
Policy v1.1

GreenBook §9.05 — Documentation Requirement
"Management documents a change assessment process for identifying, analyzing, and responding to risks related to significant changes so that the internal control system can be quickly adapted as needed to respond to significant changes as they occur."
Documenting the process isn't enough. You need to prove which rules were active when each decision was made—especially when rules change weekly.
The Structural Difference
Traditional systems show current policy. When auditors ask "was this proper?", disputes become unresolvable.

CAST proves which policy version was active at decision time— turning "he said / she said" into verifiable fact.
