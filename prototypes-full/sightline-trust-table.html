Data Trust Framework

Evaluating input reliability for PO-level financing decisions

Core Principle

Trust data from systems with skin in the game. Be skeptical of data the borrower controls.

High Trust — Can Automate

Data Point Source Trust Why We Trust It What We Can Do
Order Placed Shopify High Customer action on Shopify's platform. Shopify's business depends on accurate order data. AUTO Track sales velocity per SKU
Payment Received Shopify Payments / Stripe High Payment processor confirmed funds. Regulated, audited, legally liable for accuracy. AUTO Trigger revenue recognition, waterfall allocation
Order Fulfilled Shopify + Carrier High Tracking number from carrier. Independently verifiable via carrier API. AUTO Reduce inventory, calculate repayment due
Funds Deposited Bank / Shopify Payments High Bank confirmed deposit. Regulatory oversight, auditable records. AUTO Execute sweep to lender
Refund Issued Shopify High Money moved back to customer. Verified by payment processor. AUTO Reverse waterfall allocation, adjust repayment
Chargeback Filed Payment Processor High Bank-initiated dispute. Independent of borrower. AUTO Flag, hold allocation pending resolution

Key Insight

High-trust data enables automatic execution. The borrower never touches these inputs—they flow directly from Shopify to Sightline to the lender. This is why the repayment engine can be self-enforcing.

Medium Trust — Verify When Possible

Data Point Source Trust Why We're Cautious How to Verify
PO Issued Borrower ERP Medium Borrower could fabricate POs to obtain financing for non-existent purchases. VERIFY Require supplier confirmation or deposit payment proof
Goods Shipped Supplier Medium Supplier has incentive to claim shipment before actually shipping (to accelerate payment). VERIFY Require Bill of Lading, cross-check with carrier
Invoice Amount Supplier Medium Generally aligns with supplier's incentive (they want accurate payment). But could collude with borrower. VERIFY Match to PO, check for unusual patterns
Product Listed Shopify Catalog Medium Borrower controls catalog, but product must exist in Shopify to sell. Verifiable. VERIFY Confirm SKU exists and is active in store
Expected Retail Price Borrower / Shopify Medium Borrower could inflate expected price. But actual sale prices are verifiable. VERIFY Compare to actual historical sales data

Low Trust — Require Independent Verification

Data Point Source Trust Why We Don't Trust It Mitigation
Goods Received Borrower Low Borrower has incentive to delay acknowledging receipt (extends payment terms, delays obligation to pay). MITIGATE Require 3PL confirmation or carrier delivery proof
Inventory Count Borrower WMS Low Borrower could overstate inventory to inflate collateral value and borrowing capacity. MITIGATE 3PL integration, periodic audit, or lower advance rate
Inventory Location Borrower Low No independent verification. Goods could be anywhere (or nowhere). MITIGATE Require 3PL or bonded warehouse with API access
Inventory Condition Borrower Low Borrower could claim goods are sellable when they're damaged, expired, or obsolete. MITIGATE Age-based discounting, require photos, spot audits
Cost Basis (COGS) Borrower Low Borrower could inflate costs to claim larger advance (if advance is % of COGS). MITIGATE Require supplier invoice, payment confirmation
Sales Forecast Borrower Low Borrower is inherently optimistic. Forecasts are opinions, not facts. MITIGATE Use only historical velocity data from Shopify

The Pattern

Notice that sell-side data (Shopify, payment processors, banks) is high-trust because those systems have independent accountability. Buy-side data (borrower assertions about POs, receipts, inventory) is low-trust because the borrower has incentive to misrepresent. This shapes what we can automate vs. what requires verification.

Decision Framework

High Trust → Automate

  • • Execute without human review
  • • Real-time processing
  • • Self-enforcing contracts
  • • Lender gets continuous visibility

Medium Trust → Verify

  • • Cross-reference with other sources
  • • Require corroborating evidence
  • • Flag anomalies for review
  • • Build trust over time

Low Trust → Mitigate

  • • Require independent verification
  • • Price the risk (lower advance)
  • • Add trust anchors (3PL, audits)
  • • Never automate decisions
Strategic Implication

What This Means for Product

Build the repayment engine first. It depends only on high-trust Shopify data.

The advance decision can start with manual underwriting and lower advance rates. Add trust anchors (3PL integration, supplier onboarding) to increase automation over time.
