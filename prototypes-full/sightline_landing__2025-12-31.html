The Insight

Documentation isn't the problem. Temporal proof is.

The GreenBook requires extensive documentation. Agencies have documentation. What they don't have is proof that documentation existed before the decision it supposedly supports.

"Insufficient documentation" is the #1 cause of improper payment findings. But the real issue isn't missing paperwork—it's that systems can't distinguish between contemporaneous records and retroactive assembly.

Sightline makes this distinction architectural. Every fact carries two timestamps: when it was true, and when it was recorded. You can't fake the sequence.

How It Works

Bitemporal architecture makes fraud structurally harder

01
Every fact carries two timestamps
Valid time (when the fact was true in the world) and transaction time (when the system recorded it). You can't alter historical state without creating a new record with a new transaction time.
02
Decision basis is captured at decision time
When a payment is authorized, the system snapshots what was known: which policy was active, what verification existed, what the basis was. This can't be reconstructed later—it's recorded in real time.
03
Retroactive changes are visible, not hidden
When new information arrives (wage records, verification results), the system records it with its own transaction time. Auditors can see exactly when contradicting information became known.
04
Policy versions are timestamped too
In emergencies, eligibility rules change weekly. Sightline proves which policy version was active at each decision point—ending disputes about which rules applied when.
