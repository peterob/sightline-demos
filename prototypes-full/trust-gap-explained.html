The Trust Gap in Financial Operations

Every company runs on three parallel realities that are supposed to converge but never quite do. The monthly close is the painful ritual of forcing agreement after the fact.

The Three Parallel Realities

The Business

Owned by Operations
  • Timing: Real-time
  • Systems: Salesforce, AWS Console, Coupa, Workday
  • Truth: What actually happened — contracts signed, servers spun up, POs issued

The Ledger

Owned by Accounting
  • Timing: Monthly / Quarterly
  • Systems: NetSuite, SAP, QuickBooks
  • Truth: What we recorded — journal entries, accruals, allocations

The Cash

Owned by Treasury
  • Timing: Daily
  • Systems: Bank portals, Treasury workstations
  • Truth: What the bank saw — cleared transactions, immutable

The links between these realities are maintained by humans on different schedules. That's the gap.

Get surprised again next month

The Four Levels of Financial Truth

Irrefutable

Level 0

Cryptographic Proof

Mathematical certainty. The hash proves this exact payload existed at this exact timestamp. No human can dispute it.
Claim
"This payload existed at 2024-01-12T03:42:17Z"
Proof: SHA-256 hash + timestamp signature
Irrefutable

Level 1

System-Attested Event

A system of record confirms: this happened. You have the receipt. The bank says the cash moved. Stripe says the charge cleared.
Claim
"Chase cleared $15,000 on Jan 12"
Proof: txn_id=ACH-20240112-9928374
Refutable

Level 2

Cross-System Correlation

We believe these two events are related. The bank debit probably matches this invoice. But maybe it was a different invoice. There's room for dispute.
Claim
"This bank debit matches Invoice #4472"
Dispute: "Actually, it was Invoice #4471"
Refutable

Level 3

Human Interpretation

A narrative explanation. "Engineering didn't tell Finance." This is a story we tell ourselves, not a fact we can prove. It might be wrong.
Claim
"The AWS overage was due to unplanned scaling"
Dispute: "Actually, it was a pricing tier change"

The Problem We Solve

Today: Reconciliation After the Fact

  • Operations, Accounting, and Treasury run in parallel silos
  • Finance discovers surprises during close
  • Variance explanations are prose narratives (Level 3)
  • Forecasts are built on stale ledger data
  • Trust is asserted, not proven

With Attestation: Coordination in Real-Time

  • Every commercial event creates a cryptographic record (Level 0)
  • Systems attest to what happened, as it happens (Level 1)
  • Variance is pure math: Actual − Forecast = Gap
  • Cause is only asserted when we have the attestation chain
  • Trust is proven, not asserted
