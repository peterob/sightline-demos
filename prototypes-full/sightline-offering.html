The Offering

Sightline provides PO-level inventory financing where each purchase order is financed as an isolated silo. Unlike traditional inventory lines that use a blanket lien, each PO stands alone—a slow-selling SKU doesn't contaminate the rest of the portfolio.

Repayment is automatic. When a customer order is fulfilled and payment is confirmed, a portion of the proceeds flows directly to the lender. No monthly reconciliation. No borrower-reported data. The entire repayment loop runs on verified data from Shopify, the 3PL, and the bank.

Data Trust Framework

The core principle: Trust data from systems with skin in the game. Be skeptical of data the borrower controls.

Why 3PL changes everything: When finished goods ship directly to a 3PL and customer orders fulfill from that 3PL, the borrower never touches the inventory. Receipt, storage, count, and shipment are verified by an independent party with contractual liability.

Per-Sale Economics

While the loan is outstanding, each sale triggers a waterfall allocation. The lender receives a fixed percentage of the gross margin until the advance is repaid.

Line Item Amount % of Sale
Sale Price $99.00 100%
COGS (pre-funded) ($45.00) 45%
Gross Margin $54.00 55%
→ To Lender (repayment) ($27.00) 27%
→ To Borrower (margin) $27.00 27%

After the advance is fully repaid, 100% of the margin flows to the borrower. The faster inventory sells, the sooner the borrower captures full margin.

Risk Isolation

In traditional inventory financing, a slow-moving SKU affects the entire borrowing base. If 20% of inventory stops selling, the borrower may face a covenant breach that restricts all future borrowing.

With PO-level financing, each purchase order is a separate silo. If PO-003 underperforms, it doesn't affect PO-001, PO-002, PO-004, or PO-005. The lender's exposure is granular. The borrower's other positions are unaffected.

This isolation enables experimentation. A borrower can finance a new product launch or test a new channel without risking their core inventory line.
