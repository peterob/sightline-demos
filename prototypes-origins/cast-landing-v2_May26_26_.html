The problem

Every payment today is one-sided.

The buyer approves an invoice internally. The seller learns of the payment only when money arrives — or when something goes wrong. Neither side has a record the other co-signed.

Today
✗Buyer approves the payment. Seller is not asked.

How it works

Four steps. One shared record.

CAST sits alongside your existing accounting system. It does not replace your ERP. It adds one confirmation step before covered payments are released.

01

Payment is flagged

Your rules identify payments that need both parties to confirm — new vendors, bank-account changes, high-value amounts, rush payments, or duplicate invoices. Everything else passes through normally.

02

Seller receives a secure link

The seller gets a single link showing the exact payment details: amount, invoice number, payment date, and the last four digits of the destination account. No account setup. No app to install.

03 ·

Seller confirms, requests a change, or disputes

The seller chooses one of three actions. Their response is cryptographically signed to the exact payment details and time-stamped. This is the shared record.

The pivot — payment stays held until this happens
04

Payment releases with proof attached

Once the seller confirms, the payment is released. The signed record travels with it — into your audit trail, your lender's review, and the seller's own records. Both sides hold the same document.

Security

Fraud that works today stops working.

Most payment fraud succeeds because only one party needs to be deceived. CAST requires both parties to confirm. An attacker who controls one inbox, one email address, or one phone call cannot produce a bilateral confirmation.

What gets covered

Start where the risk is highest.

You define which payments require confirmation. These are the six most common starting points. Everything below your thresholds passes through without interruption.

First payment to a new vendor

No relationship yet. Both parties confirm the destination before funds move.

Bank-account change

Any destination update triggers confirmation. The seller sees the account number before release.

High-value payment

Payments above your threshold require a bilateral confirmation before proceeding.

Rush payment

Urgency does not bypass controls. Rush payments still wait for the seller to confirm.

Duplicate invoice risk

Potential duplicates are held for confirmation or review before money moves.

Seller non-response

If the seller does not respond in time, the payment stays held. The exception is recorded.
