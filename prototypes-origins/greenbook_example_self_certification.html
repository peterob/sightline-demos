GreenBook §8.12 & Appendix II — Self-Certification Risk

When Was Self-Certification Verified?

The GreenBook identifies "the extent to which the entity relies on potential recipients self-certifying their own eligibility" as an improper payment risk factor. The question isn't whether you verified—it's when.
Scenario: Emergency Unemployment Claim
Applicant self-certifies: "I was employed at ABC Corp and lost my job on March 10, 2024."

The agency must verify this claim against employer wage records. But wage records arrive quarterly— weeks or months after benefits are paid. This is the verification timing gap that enabled billions in COVID-era fraud.

GreenBook Appendix II — Detective Control Activities
"For self-certified information that was relied on to determine eligibility or identity, management may subsequently collect supporting documentation and conduct a postpayment review."
The GreenBook acknowledges verification often happens after payment. The question is: can you prove what you knew at decision time?
The Structural Difference
Traditional systems know that self-certification was later contradicted.

CAST proves what verification existed at payment time— and makes the decision basis permanently auditable.
