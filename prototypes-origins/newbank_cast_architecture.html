01 Borrower context — why Crate & Barrel fits The structural pain points CAST addresses

Crate & Barrel operates three retail brands (Crate & Barrel, CB2, Hudson Grace) with a heavy import supply chain across Asia and Europe. Seasonal inventory build peaks August–October ahead of Q4. Their ABL facility is structured against inventory and AR — both of which carry the standard problem: every number in the borrowing base certificate is borrower-reported.

Pain point 01
Monthly certificate lag

Borrowing base certified monthly via spreadsheet. NewBank's credit team reconciles against aged inventory reports 3–4 days after period close. The facility is over- or under-drawn for the entire interim.

~$800K annual cost of carry inefficiency
Pain point 02
In-transit inventory haircut

Goods in transit between Asian manufacturers and Chicago DCs are either excluded from the borrowing base or haircut to 40–50% because the lender cannot verify location, condition, or title.

~$12M excluded collateral at peak season
Pain point 03
Multi-brand concentration

CB2 and Crate & Barrel share suppliers and warehouse infrastructure. Cross-brand concentration exposure is invisible in monthly certificate reporting — it only surfaces at audit.

Hidden covenant risk at peak

04 Sightline — real-time borrowing base Continuous vs monthly certificate model

Under the traditional model, C&B submits a borrowing base certificate monthly. NewBank receives it 3–4 days after period close, reconciles, and confirms availability. The facility is effectively unmanaged for most of the month. Under CAST/Sightline, the borrowing base is a live calculation derived from verified events — updated on every bilateral confirmation.

05 AI agent use cases Where structured event state enables order-of-magnitude leverage
BB
Continuous borrowing base agent

Recalculates the borrowing base on every bilateral event confirmation. When a GOODS_RECEIVED event posts a 60-unit variance, the agent immediately adjusts eligible collateral, checks whether the variance breaches policy tolerance, and either absorbs it (within tolerance) or issues an availability reduction notice to C&B treasury.

Trigger: Any bilateral event that changes collateral composition or value
Output: Updated availability, draw limit, variance notice if required
CV
Covenant proximity monitor

Tracks concentration limits across C&B's three brands continuously. When CB2 and Crate & Barrel both draw against the same supplier's inventory, the agent detects the concentration event and compares it against the policy version's limits — before it becomes a covenant violation. Flags to NewBank's credit team with 72-hour advance notice.

Trigger: Cross-brand supplier concentration approaching 25% limit
Output: Alert to credit team, draft covenant waiver memo if breached
FA
Field exam automation agent

Traditional ABL field exams require a credit analyst to spend 3–5 days on-site reconciling borrower records against the borrowing base. With CAST, the bitemporal event log is the field exam. The agent prepares the exam report from structured state — every inventory count, every receipt event, every variance — before the examiner arrives. On-site time drops to verification of the AI-prepared report.

Trigger: Scheduled field exam or covenant trigger
Output: Pre-populated exam report, variance analysis, exception list
VA
Variance pattern agent

Across C&B's import PO portfolio, receipt variances follow patterns — specific suppliers, specific shipping lanes, specific seasons. The agent analyzes variance history across all bilateral receipt events, identifies suppliers with consistent short-shipment patterns, and feeds this into advance rate recommendations. A supplier with a 2% average variance gets a policy-version advance rate adjustment at next renewal.

Trigger: Quarterly portfolio review or new supplier onboarding
Output: Supplier risk scores, advance rate recommendations, policy version update memo
