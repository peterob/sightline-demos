2. Feature Tier Classification

Based on prevalence and strategic importance, features should be tiered:

🟢 Tier 1: Core (Must Have in Every Prototype)

  • KPI Cards (88%) — The universal "at-a-glance" pattern. Every stakeholder expects instant metrics.
  • Data Tables (80%) — The workhorse of detail views. Essential for operational users.
  • Tab System (76%) — Primary navigation within views. Enables progressive disclosure.
  • Charts/Viz (72%) — Visual storytelling is expected in modern dashboards.

🟡 Tier 2: Standard (Include Unless Scope-Specific)

  • Event Stream (72%) — Your differentiator. Shows real-time activity and builds trust.
  • Approval Flows (60%) — Critical for operational prototypes. Skip only for read-only dashboards.
  • Policy Engine (56%) — Demonstrates configurability. Key for enterprise sales.
  • Hash Chain/Audit (56%) — Your technical moat. Include visual proof of immutability.

🟣 Tier 3: Differentiator (Strategic Emphasis Needed)

  • AI/Agents (32%) — Underrepresented given market positioning. Needs more prominence.
  • Forecasting (32%) — Key value prop but only in 8 prototypes. Should be elevated.
  • Sidebar Navigation (40%) — Inconsistent implementation creates UX fragmentation.

3. Critical Gaps & Design Implications

⚠️ Gap 1: AI/Agents Are Undersold (32%)

Your narrative emphasizes autonomous agents, but only 8 of 25 prototypes showcase them prominently. This creates a disconnect between marketing promise and demo reality.

Design Fix: Add an "Agent Activity" indicator or panel to every operational prototype. Even a simple "3 items auto-processed by AP Agent" callout reinforces the AI story.

⚠️ Gap 2: Forecasting Is Hidden (32%)

The 13-week forecast is a headline feature, but it only appears fully in 8 prototypes. CFOs expect forecasting; it shouldn't require navigation to a specialized view.

Design Fix: Add a "Forecast Snapshot" widget to every dashboard showing next 4 weeks with confidence indicators. Make forecasting omnipresent, not a destination.

⚠️ Gap 3: Navigation Inconsistency (40% Full Sidebar)

Only 10 prototypes have full sidebar navigation. This creates a fragmented experience when demoing multiple prototypes in sequence—users get lost.

Design Fix: Standardize on a collapsible sidebar pattern with consistent section groups (Overview, Operations, Configuration). Apply to all operational prototypes.

4. Strategic Design Recommendations

📐 Recommendation 1: Create a "Core Shell" Template

Build one master HTML template with: fixed sidebar, top header with global KPIs, tab container, and event stream footer. All new prototypes inherit this shell, ensuring consistency and faster development.

📐 Recommendation 2: Standardize the "Trust Strip"

Every prototype should include a horizontal strip showing: last sync time, hash verification status, and active policy count. This reinforces your technical differentiation without requiring a dedicated Trust Architecture view.

📐 Recommendation 3: Embed Agent Presence Everywhere

Add a persistent "Agent Status" indicator (similar to a notification badge) showing: "AP Agent: 12 items processed today" or "Collections Agent: 2 escalations pending." Agents should feel active, not hidden in configuration.

📐 Recommendation 4: Collapse Vertical Variants into Configurable Dashboards

You have 5 CAST dashboards (CFO, HOA, RBF, Dental, SaaS) with 70-85% feature overlap. Consider a single "CAST Dashboard" prototype with a vertical selector dropdown that swaps terminology and sample data. This reduces maintenance and demonstrates platform flexibility.

💡 The 80/20 Insight

Four features appear in 70%+ of prototypes: KPI Cards, Data Tables, Tabs, and Charts. These form the universal grammar of your product. Invest in perfecting these four patterns once, then reuse everywhere.

Three features are underrepresented despite strategic importance: AI Agents, Forecasting, and Consistent Navigation. These need intentional elevation in your next design iteration.

7. Summary: What This Means for Your Product

Key Takeaways

  1. Your prototypes have converged on a pattern — KPI cards + tables + tabs + charts. This is your product's visual language. Standardize it.
  2. Event streams are your signature — 72% prevalence means users expect it. Make it the hero, not a sidebar feature.
  3. AI and forecasting are under-demonstrated — These are your strategic differentiators but appear in only 32% of prototypes. Elevate them.
  4. Vertical variants share 70-85% DNA — Consolidate into configurable dashboards to reduce maintenance and demonstrate platform flexibility.
  5. Navigation inconsistency hurts demos — A prospect seeing Landing → Demo → CFO Dashboard experiences three different navigation paradigms. Fix this.
